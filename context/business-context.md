# Business Context: Dreamforce Staffing

## What is Dreamforce?

Dreamforce is Salesforce's annual flagship conference held in San Francisco. It's one of the largest enterprise tech conferences in the world with ~100,000+ attendees (pre-pandemic).

**2026 Event Dates:** September 15-17, 2026

## Slack's Presence at Dreamforce

Slack (owned by Salesforce) has a significant presence at Dreamforce:

- **Campground booth(s)** - Main booth area for demos and conversations
- **SICs (Strategic Innovation Centers)** - Dedicated demo spaces
- **Spoken sessions** - Presentations to conference attendees
- **Client meetings** - 1:1 or small group meetings with customers
- **Executive presence** - Salesforce/Slack executives presenting and networking

### Why Slack Participates

- **Brand visibility** - Showcase Slack to Salesforce ecosystem
- **Customer engagement** - Meet and support existing customers
- **Lead generation** - Connect with prospects
- **Product marketing** - Launch and promote new features
- **Partner relationships** - Strengthen relationships with Salesforce and partners
- **Employee engagement** - Rally team around company presence

## Business Drivers & Constraints

### Why Staffing Matters

**Business Impact of Good Staffing:**
- Quality booth experience → better brand perception
- Right people at right time → more qualified leads
- Demo specialists available → better product demonstration
- Executives available → stronger partnerships and deals

**Business Impact of Poor Staffing:**
- Under-staffed booth → long wait times, poor experience
- Wrong people scheduled → can't answer questions effectively
- Last-minute gaps → stressed team, scrambling for coverage
- Over-allocation of passes → wasted expense

### Pass Scarcity

Dreamforce passes are expensive and limited:
- Not everyone who wants to go can go
- Orgs compete for limited pass allocations
- Must justify pass allocation with staffing needs
- Over-allocating passes is wasteful
- Under-allocating means under-staffed events

**This creates tension:**
- People want to attend for professional development
- Leaders want their teams visible at the event
- But passes must be tied to actual staffing needs
- Staffing lead must balance all these interests

### Budget Considerations

- **Pass costs:** Expensive (thousands per pass)
- **Travel and lodging:** SF is expensive, limited availability
- **Opportunity cost:** People away from their day jobs
- **Vendor costs:** Booth, sessions, materials

**Implication:** Efficiency matters. Reducing manual hours and improving accuracy has real business value.

## Risk Context

### What Could Go Wrong

**Under-staffing:**
- Booth unstaffed during peak hours
- Sessions without presenters
- Client meetings with no Slack rep
- Bad attendee experience

**Over-allocation:**
- Wasted passes and money
- People attending without clear role
- Org leaders frustrated about pass decisions

**Process Failure:**
- Summer overwhelmed and can't keep up
- Last-minute scrambling like last year
- Data mistakes (double-booking, lost assignments)
- People show up without knowing their assignments

**Reputational:**
- Poor experience reflects badly on Slack
- Internal team frustration and burnout
- Leaders lose confidence in staffing process
