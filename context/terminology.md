# Terminology: Dreamforce Staffing

## Event Terms

### Dreamforce
Salesforce's annual flagship conference in San Francisco. One of the largest enterprise tech conferences with 100,000+ attendees.

### Event Footprint
The physical presence Slack has at Dreamforce - includes booths, sessions, meeting spaces, SICs. Determined by Strategic Events team.

### Campground
Main expo hall area where booths are located. Primary area for informal demos and conversations with attendees.

### SIC (Strategic Innovation Center)
Dedicated demo space, usually more formal than booth. Often themed around specific product capabilities or use cases.

### Session
A scheduled presentation or talk given to conference attendees. Includes spoken sessions, panels, workshops.

### Spoken Session
A presentation with a speaker on stage talking to an audience. Different from booth conversations or demos.

### Client Meeting
1:1 or small group meeting between Slack/Salesforce staff and customers or prospects. Often scheduled in advance.

## Role Terms

### Staffing Lead
The person responsible for overall coordination of Slack's Dreamforce staffing. Summer Hash for 2026, you for 2025.

### Event Floor Lead
Person managing on-the-ground operations during the event. Dustin Burnett is this role.

### Session Owner
Individual responsible for a specific session's content and logistics. Often a PMM or product leader. Manages their own session staffing.

### Demo Specialist
Professional role (usually from Marika's team) focused on giving product demos. More skilled/trained than general volunteers.

### Booth Staff / Volunteer
Slack employees who volunteer to work shifts at the booth talking to attendees.

### Producer
Someone managing logistics of the event (not staffing a booth or giving a session). Behind-the-scenes operational role.

### Executive
C-level or VP-level leader. At Dreamforce for keynotes, press, strategic meetings, not typically doing booth shifts.

## Pass & Allocation Terms

### Pass
Ticket/credential to attend Dreamforce. Expensive and limited. Required to access event.

### Pass Allocation
How many passes each Slack org (marketing, product, sales, etc.) receives. Decided by org leaders.

### Pass Allocator
Person deciding who gets passes within their org. James Turner (Ryan Gavin's org), Ria Mancoosh (Rob Seaman's org).

### Apply
Employee requests a pass to attend Dreamforce (usually because they want to staff).

### Approve
Leader says yes to their direct report's pass application.

### Assign
Pass allocator gives a pass to an approved applicant.

### Volunteer
Employee who has a pass and signs up to staff booth shifts (via Staffing Force).

## Staffing Process Terms

### Staffing Requirements
How many people are needed for each area/shift, and what roles/skills they need.

### Shift
Time block for booth/area coverage. Example: "Tuesday 10am-2pm campground booth."

### Shift Signup
When employee volunteers for a specific shift via Staffing Force.

### Shift Swap
When one person can't make their shift and another person takes it instead.

### Cancellation
When someone drops out of a shift with no immediate replacement.

### Last-Minute Changes
The chaos of people canceling, swapping, and requesting changes in the days/hours before and during the event.

### Under-Staffed
Shift or area that doesn't have enough people signed up. Risk of gaps in coverage.

### Over-Allocated
Giving out more passes than there are actual staffing needs. Wasteful.

### Coverage Gap
Time period where a booth or session has no one staffed. Very bad outcome.

## System Terms

### Rainfocus
Backend event management system used by Salesforce. Official system of record but hard to use.

### Staffing Force
Employee-facing interface for volunteering for event staffing. Front-end to Rainfocus for booth shifts.

### Export
Pulling data out of Rainfocus (likely as CSV or Excel). Probably manual process.

### Source of Truth
The definitive, authoritative place where information lives. Often unclear in practice.

### Shadow System
Unofficial system (usually spreadsheets) used because official system is hard to use.

### Sync
Keeping multiple systems up to date with the same data. Often manual in current process.

## Jobs-to-Be-Done Terms

### JTBD (Jobs-to-Be-Done)
Framework for understanding what someone needs to accomplish (outcome) rather than just activities they do.

### Automation Opportunity
Task or job that could potentially be automated or AI-assisted to reduce manual effort.

### Pain Point
Aspect of current process that is time-consuming, error-prone, or frustrating.

### Manual Work
Tasks requiring human data entry, copy/paste, coordination, etc. Target for automation.

### Self-Service
Capability for people to handle their own requests (shift swaps, cancellations) without staffing lead intervention.

### Quick Win
Automation that's easy to build and has immediate, obvious value.

### High-ROI Automation
Automation that saves significant time/effort relative to complexity of building it.

## Phrases & Idioms

### "Horror Show"
Your description of last-minute changes last year. Chaotic, stressful, barely manageable.

### "Source of Truth"
Definitive location for information. Often aspirational rather than actual.

### "Rainfocus is Hard to Use"
Understated way of saying the system is nearly unusable, causing people to create workarounds.

### "Made Up Based on Past Experience"
How staffing requirements are determined currently. Tribal knowledge, not documented formulas.

### "Shadow Spreadsheets"
Unofficial spreadsheets created because official system doesn't work well.

### "Manual Sync"
Person copying data from one system to another by hand. Error-prone and time-consuming.

### "Last-Minute Fire Drill"
Scrambling to handle problems in the days/hours before or during event.

### "Self-Service"
People solving their own problems without needing staffing lead to coordinate.

## Context-Specific Meanings

### "Session" (Multiple Meanings)
Could mean:
1. A spoken presentation at the conference
2. A demo in a SIC
3. A time period for analysis
**Clarify from context which meaning is intended.**

### "Staff" (Verb vs. Noun)
- **Verb:** To provide people for booth/session (e.g., "We need to staff the booth")
- **Noun:** The people who work at booth/session (e.g., "The staff were great")

### "Pass" (Multiple Meanings)
1. Conference ticket/credential
2. To give something to someone ("pass allocation")
3. To approve something
**Clarify from context.**

### "Owner"
Not ownership in business sense, but person responsible for something (e.g., "session owner" = responsible for that session).
