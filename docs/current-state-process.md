## Detailed Process Flows

### Phase 1: Planning

#### Step 1.1: Get Event Footprint Information

**Actors:** Strategic Events → Staffing Lead

**Flow:**
1. Strategic Events plans Slack's presence (booths, SICs, sessions)
2. Works with external vendor on booth design
3. Information shared to... someone? (Alyssa? Event lead?)
4. Eventually trickles down to staffing lead
5. Information comes via: docs, emails, meetings, conversations

**Systems:** Unknown document location, email, Slack

**Pain Points:**
- Unclear where source of truth is
- Information feels gated or slow
- Hard to know when decisions are final
- Feels like working in the dark

**Time:** 2-4 hours initially + ongoing monitoring

---

#### Step 1.2: Calculate Staffing Needs

**Actor:** Staffing Lead (with input from Dustin)

**Flow:**
1. Review event footprint (what's being built)
2. Apply tribal knowledge: "booth needs X people per shift"
3. Consider: booth hours, SIC schedules, sessions
4. Create spreadsheet with requirements
5. "Made up based on what worked before"
6. Consult Dustin for validation

**Systems:** Google Sheets

**Pain Points:**
- No documented formulas, all in your/Dustin's head
- Manual spreadsheet creation
- Hard to explain/justify numbers
- Easy to under or over-estimate

**Time:** 4-8 hours initially + revisions

---

#### Step 1.3: Model Pass Allocations

**Actor:** Staffing Lead

**Flow:**
1. Look at staffing requirements by role
2. Consider which orgs have people with needed skills
3. Factor in: some roles can be non-Slack people
4. Create allocation model in spreadsheet
5. Cross-reference pass requests with needs
6. Prepare recommendations

**Systems:** Google Sheets

**Pain Points:**
- Complex manual cross-referencing
- Hard to model scenarios
- Allocation is politically sensitive
- Time-consuming to balance

**Time:** 6-10 hours for initial model

---

#### Step 1.4: Present Allocations

**Actor:** Staffing Lead → Pass Allocators (James, Ria)

**Flow:**
1. Present recommended allocations
2. James (Ryan Gavin's org) and Ria (Rob Seaman's org) review
3. Negotiate and adjust based on org priorities
4. Finalize pass allocations by org

**Systems:** Meetings, docs, email

**Pain Points:**
- Political negotiation
- Competing priorities
- May need multiple rounds

**Time:** Multiple meetings

### Phase 3: Scheduling

#### Step 3.1: Set Up Shifts

**Actor:** Staffing Lead (possibly with Marika's team)

**Flow:**
1. Create shifts in Rainfocus / Staffing Force
2. For each shift define: date, time, location, role, # needed
3. Probably manual data entry for many shifts

**Systems:** Rainfocus / Staffing Force

**Pain Points:**
- Manual data entry, tedious
- Easy to make mistakes
- Hard to edit if schedule changes

**Time:** 3-6 hours for initial setup

---

#### Step 3.2: Announce Shift Signups

**Actor:** Staffing Lead → Pass Holders

**Flow:**
1. Email everyone who has a pass
2. Explain how to use Staffing Force
3. Set deadline for signups
4. Answer individual questions

**Systems:** Email, Slack

**Pain Points:**
- People don't read instructions
- Repeated questions
- Confusion about system

**Time:** 2-4 hours + ongoing Q&A

---

#### Step 3.3: Monitor Shift Signups

**Actor:** Staffing Lead

**Flow:**
1. Log into Rainfocus / Staffing Force
2. Check each shift's signup count
3. Compare to requirements
4. Identify under-staffed shifts
5. Track in spreadsheet (because Rainfocus is hard to use)

**Systems:** Rainfocus + Spreadsheet

**Pain Points:**
- Manual checking, very time-consuming
- Rainfocus interface is hard to use
- Hard to get overview
- Only know gaps when you check
- People create shadow spreadsheets

**Time:** 1-2 hours per day during signup period (10-20 hours total)

---

#### Step 3.4: Recruit for Gaps

**Actor:** Staffing Lead

**Flow:**
1. Identify under-staffed shifts
2. Find pass holders who haven't signed up yet
3. Reach out individually via Slack or email
4. Ask if they can cover specific shifts
5. Follow up on non-responses
6. Update spreadsheet when they commit
7. Manually update Rainfocus

**Systems:** Slack, Email, Spreadsheet, Rainfocus

**Pain Points:**
- Manual outreach one-by-one
- Tracking who you've asked
- Follow-ups are tedious
- Multiple systems to update

**Time:** 5-10 hours for recruiting

---

#### Step 3.5: Session Staffing (Parallel)

**Actors:** Session Owners (PMMs, product leaders)

**Flow:**
1. Session owners manage their own staffing
2. Use their own spreadsheets, calendars, etc.
3. Coordinate with Marika's team for demo specialists
4. Not centrally managed by staffing lead
5. Risk of conflicts (demo specialist double-booked)

**Systems:** Session owner's tools (varied), Google Calendar

**Pain Points:**
- No central visibility
- Potential conflicts between sessions and booth
- Demo specialists overcommitted
- Hard to coordinate

**Time:** N/A for staffing lead (unless conflicts arise)

---

### Phase 4: Finalization

#### Step 4.1: Confirm Assignments

**Actor:** Staffing Lead → All Staff

**Flow:**
1. Export final assignments from Rainfocus
2. Create confirmation emails
3. Send personalized emails with shift details
4. Include: times, locations, what to bring
5. Create calendar invites
6. Answer questions

**Systems:** Rainfocus export, Email, Google Calendar

**Pain Points:**
- Manual email composition
- Info changes and needs resending
- People lose emails

**Time:** 3-5 hours

---

#### Step 4.2: Handle Last-Minute Cancellations

**Actor:** Staffing Lead ← Staff

**Flow:**
1. Person DMs staffing lead: "I can't make my shift"
2. Staffing lead searches for replacement
3. Posts in Slack channel or DMs individuals
4. Coordinates until replacement found
5. Manually updates Rainfocus
6. Confirms with both people

**Systems:** Slack DMs, Rainfocus

**Pain Points:**
- **"HORROR SHOW"**
- Manual coordination, bottleneck
- Frantic searching for coverage
- Person can't self-service
- Rainfocus updates are slow and manual
- Stressful and time-consuming

**Time:** 2-5 hours in final week, escalating toward event

---

#### Step 4.3: Handle Shift Swaps

**Actor:** Staffing Lead

**Flow:**
1. Person A: "Can someone swap with me?"
2. Staffing lead helps find Person B
3. Verify both are qualified for each other's shifts
4. Coordinate the swap
5. Manually update Rainfocus
6. Confirm with both people

**Systems:** Slack, Rainfocus

**Pain Points:**
- Same as cancellations
- People can't coordinate themselves
- Manual and slow

**Time:** 1-3 hours in final week

---

### Phase 5: Execution (During Event)

#### Step 5.1: Monitor Coverage

**Actors:** Staffing Lead, Dustin (Floor Lead)

**Flow:**
1. Staffing lead checks in with Dustin
2. Dustin reports if shifts are covered
3. Respond to messages about no-shows
4. Visible presence to monitor

**Systems:** In-person, Slack

**Pain Points:**
- Hard to know status remotely
- Reactive, only know problems when reported

**Time:** Ongoing during event

---

#### Step 5.2: Handle Day-Of Emergencies

**Actors:** Staffing Lead, Dustin

**Flow:**
1. Get alert about no-show or emergency
2. Find anyone available on-site
3. In-person or Slack/text recruitment
4. Fill gap quickly

**Systems:** In-person, Slack

**Pain Points:**
- High-stress, time-sensitive
- Limited pool of available people

**Time:** Variable, hopefully rare

## Pain Point Summary

### Top 5 Pain Points by Impact

1. **Last-minute changes are a "horror show"**
   - No self-service
   - Manual coordination bottleneck
   - Stressful and time-consuming

2. **Unclear event footprint and source of truth**
   - Hard to plan without complete picture
   - Information feels gated

3. **Manual monitoring of shift signups**
   - Time-consuming daily checking
   - Rainfocus is hard to use
   - No overview or alerts

4. **System fragmentation and manual sync**
   - Rainfocus ↔ Spreadsheet manual sync
   - Multiple sources of truth
   - Error-prone

5. **Complex pass allocation modeling**
   - Manual cross-referencing
   - Hard to model scenarios
   - Time-consuming
